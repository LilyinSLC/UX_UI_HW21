Grow Orchids in Utah

Yes, you can grow orchids at home in Utah like a pro.
It is an easy, beautiful, and therapeutic thing to do.

GET STARTED

Be Patient

An orchid is a slow-growing plant. The time from budding to blomming can take months. But it's worth the wait. Your patience will be paid off with beautiful blooms that last a long time. The biggest bud will open first, then one by one, they will all bloom.

Doesn't Need to be Expensive

You don't need to spend a lot of money to get your first orchid plant. You can ask as a gift on your next birthday, or look at places such as Walmart to get a small one. The orchid shown above was bought 4 years ago at Walmart for $11.00. It has bloomed every year since then.

Location, Location, Location

This is the most important thing to consider. Orchids like bright sunny spots but not direct sunlight. Windows facing East will be the best. My bathroom window just happens to face the east. It gets morning sun for about 3 hours. (Warning - watch out for the heat. I have to tape a light scarf on the window glass during May and June when the sun gets too hot.)

Add Some Humidity

Orchid likes humidity. Utah is very dry. Normally the bathroom is ideal because of the moisure from the shower. But this bathroom has nobody uses it so I have to buy a humidifier to make sure my orchids get the moisture they need.

TIPS AND TRICKS

Growing orchids at your home in Utah is actually easier than you think. Follow the tips here, you will have orchids blooming for you year after year.

  • Summer: I water them with the tap water over the sink really well (like giving them a good shower once a week.)
  • Winter: Twice a week, I add two pieces ice-cubes to each pot at the top of the pot where the new roots are.
  • If your orchid needs moisure, make sure you run the humidifier every other day.
  • Give your orchid plant a little food every two weeks. (1-teaspoon All Purpose MiracleGro mixing with half gallon water)
  • Repot ONLY after the plant finishes blooming and the plant has 4 or 5 new roots growing outside the pot.


OTHER PLANTS

There are some other plants I like to grow at home, that are very easy, and produce orchid-similar flowers. If you are still not quite sure about orchids, try these plants.

Orchid Cactus

Orchid cactus is an easy-to-grow plant with beautiful flowers in spring.

It tolerates dry air and loves water but not overwater. Water the plant when the potting mix dries. Just like orchids, it likes bright light but not direct sunlight.

Visit this site for more information about how to take care of this plant: Guide to House Plan-Orchid Cactus Care

Clivia

Clivia plants are native to South Africa. Most of the time it grows as a house-plant. It's common name is the Bush Lily.

This evergreen plant has beautiful leaves even without flowers. It is a plant that likes rich, well-draining potting mix.

I take care of mine the same way as I care for my orchids. It normally blooms once a year in spring.

My plant has bloomed twice (once in spring and later in the fall) in 2020 and again in 2021.

Visit this site if you want to know more about how to grow and take care of Clivia: How to Grow Clivia

MY FINAL THOUGHTS

Growing orchids is a passion for me. I went to an orchid show years ago with a friend of mine and got hooked on them right away. I just couldn't resist the beautiul flowers that come with so many different colors, sizes, and shapes. Once you fall in love with them, the passion runs in your blood. Like any hobby, it could be very addictive and become very expensive. As long as you know your budget - with little investment - you can get the same rewards as I have. You can have the same beautiful flowers in just a little plastic pot. All of my plants are sitting on my bathroom windowsill. I will put the blooming plant in a nicer pot on the table to display it.
